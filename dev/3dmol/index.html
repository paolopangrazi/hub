⚛️ 3D molecular viewer

Browse a library of ~3,300 drug molecules and inspect each one in interactive 3D, alongside its descriptors, chemical notations and MACCS fingerprint.

Pick a molecule

  • Molecule dropdown (top-left) — jump to any molecule by name.
  • Similar molecules (left pane) — click a row to load a structurally related molecule, ranked by Tanimoto similarity.

Move the 3D view

  • Rotate — drag with the left mouse button.
  • Zoom — scroll wheel (or pinch on a trackpad).
  • Pan — right-drag, or drag with two fingers.
  • Atom info — click any atom to open its element card.

Toolbar

  • Wireframe / Stick / Ball & stick / Space-filling — switch the render style.
  • Spin — auto-rotate the molecule; Reset view re-centres it.
  • Toggle labels — show element symbols on every atom.
  • 🌙 / ☀️ — switch between dark and light themes.

Side & bottom panels

The tables in the panels are interactive — hover for explanations and click to highlight the matching atoms on the 3D model.

  • Left · MACCS fingerprint — a 166-cell grid of structural keys; a filled cell means the molecule contains that feature.
    • Hover a cell → its key number, a plain-English description and the SMARTS pattern.
    • Click a filled cell → highlights every atom of that substructure on the model in purple; click again to clear it.
  • Left · Similar molecules — the 10 closest molecules by Tanimoto similarity, each with a bar and score. Click a row to load that molecule.
  • Right · Descriptors — size, LogP, H-bond counts, drug-likeness, clinical data…
    • Hover the * beside a label → a definition of that property.
    • Click the Aromatic rings, Rotatable bonds, H-bond donors or H-bond acceptors rows → colours those atoms on the model (each a different colour; several can be on at once). Click again to turn a highlight off.
    • Hover the Ro5 violations badge → the exact rows breaking a Lipinski rule flash red.
    • The ChEMBL link at the bottom opens the full compound record.
  • Bottom · Notations — the SMILES and SELFIES strings; hover their * for definitions and use the copy button to grab either one.
  • Collapse any panel with its arrow handle to give the viewer more room; click the thin rail to bring it back.
